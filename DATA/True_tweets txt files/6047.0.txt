U.S. Secretary of Defense James Mattis and Homeland Security chief John Kelly will attend the Munich Security Conference in February, and the event’s organizers said on Wednesday they expect other senior U.S. officials to attend also.  Mattis and Kelly were confirmed in their posts just days ago, following President Donald Trump’s Jan. 20 inauguration.  “All signs point to a particularly strong participation of both the new administration, as well as Congress,” the group said in a statement.     Bavarian state premier Horst Seehofer had invited Trump to visit Munich
