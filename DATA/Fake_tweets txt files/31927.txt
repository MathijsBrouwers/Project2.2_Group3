Discussions about the money supply can get very complicated, and that can cause people to tune out, but it doesn‚Äôt have to be that way.

That is why what is happening in the U.S. right now is so troubling.

A measure of the money supply that includes all physical money, such as coins and currency, as well as demand deposits, checking accounts and Negotiable Order of Withdrawal (NOW) accounts. It does not contain ‚Äúnear money‚Äù or ‚Äúnear, near money‚Äù as M2 and M3 do.

Next, let‚Äôs take a look at M2. It includes more things in the money supply. The following is how Investopedia defines M2‚Ä¶ 
A measure of money supply that includes cash and checking deposits (M1) as well as near money. ‚ÄúNear money‚Äù in M2 includes savings deposits, money market mutual funds and other time deposits, which are less liquid and not as suitable as exchange mediums but can be quickly converted into cash or checking deposits.
